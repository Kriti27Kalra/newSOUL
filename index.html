About SOUL

Society of Universe Lovers (SOUL) is a NGO registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860. The work under the aegis of SOUL started informally in December 2012 with a school for under-privileged children and a shelter for abandoned cows in Noida. With support, the initiatives grew, which led to the formation of the NGO and it was registered on 19 September 2013.

SOUL was founded by socially conscious people. It has grown as a movement over the years and many renowned and philanthropic souls support this movement of educating the poor tribal kids. SOUL’s areas of intervention are ‘Education for All’ under which the SOUL School named SOUL Gurukul is being run and ‘Women Empowerment’ under which Shakti Kendra is being run. Today, SOUL is a well-known and respected NGO in India.

SOUL of Empowerment and Growth

SOUL NGO is driving change through the spirit of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), with a strong focus on women empowerment and holistic community growth. Through our Fem Force initiative, we provide training in cooking and sewing, enabling women to achieve financial independence and uplift their families. With SOUL Gurukul, we ensure that children receive quality education and equal opportunities for a brighter tomorrow. Our Mid-Day Meal campaigns nourish young minds, helping them learn and grow without the burden of hunger. Additionally, our commitment extends to compassion for animals through our cow shelters, where care and dignity are provided.

Every step we take is a step toward breaking cycles of poverty, promoting equality, and building sustainable futures. Our mission echoes the essence of the SDGs—where no woman, child, or being is left behind, and where empowerment, education, and compassion walk hand in hand to create an inclusive tomorrow.

Impact Stories of SOUL

Deepanshu, a very rebellious child was always picking fights in SOUL Gurukul. After 4 years, he has matured and settled down in academics and stood first in Class 2.

Pradeep, Raja, Sahil and Nitish wasted their early years playing the whole day. Since joining SOUL Gurukul in the year 2013, they have become young environment activists.

Daughters of petty laborers, Jyoti, Sneha, and Itisha joined SOUL Gurukul in the year 2013. In six years, their knowledge and skills have improved tremendously.
